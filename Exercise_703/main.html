Introduction


Lagos city and chief port, Lagos state, Nigeria. Until 1975 it was the capital of Lagos state, and until December 1991 it was the federal capital of Nigeria.
Ikeja replaced Lagos as the state capital, and Abuja replaced Lagos as the federal capital territory of Nigeria.
Lagos, however, remained the unofficial seat of many government agencies.
The city's population is centred on Lagos Island, in Lagos Lagoon, on the Bight of Benin in the Gulf of Guinea.
Lagos is Nigeriaâ€™s largest city and one of the largest in sub-Saharan Africa.
Geographically, Lagos is locaed in the southwest of Nigeria, on the coast of the Bight of Benin. It is bounded by the state of Ogun to the north and east, by the Bight of Benin to the south, and by the Republic of Benin to the west.
From 1914 to 1954 the area included in the state was administered by the British as part of the colony of Nigeria.

Some fun facts about Lagos

  • Lagos is home to many content creators, actors musicians and comedians
  • Lagos has the highest population of residents in Nigeria but by landmass it is smallest state in Nigeria
  • Lagos is home to a reasonable number of beaches. It has more than 9 well maintained beaches in Lagos both private and public
  • Lagos is home to the longest bridge in Nigeria and the second longest bridge in Africa. The Third mainland bridge connects lagos Island to the Mainland and measures 11.8km in length
  • Lagos is home to what is called "The floating city" - Makoko People live on the houses made of wood and canoes on the water and carry out their daily activities there. Moving around the floating city is possible only through the waterways on small boats
  • Traffic. The traffic in lagos is pretty intense as you cannot escape it. lol except if you use water trasnportation. Traffic congestion has a name common to Nigerians and we call it "Go-Slow"
  • Compared to other cities, Lagos is known in Nigeria as the city that never sleeps. Lagos roads and waterways are busy round the clock. Meanwhile, Lagos clubs and entertainment centers are typically busy on weekends
